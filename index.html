02 / WHAT YOU NEED TO DO

First login after the change — three steps

Important: on your first login after the change date, you must use "Other user" on the Windows login screen — don't just tap your existing profile.

1

Confirm the change

  • Check you've received the IT confirmation email that your username has been updated.
  • Your new username will be firstname.lastname@kry.se
2

Sign out of your computer

  • Click the Windows (Start) button.
  • Click your profile name / icon.
  • Select "Sign out".
[ Screenshot: Start menu → Sign out ]
3

Sign in with your new username

  • On the login screen, look at the bottom-left.
  • Click "Other user".
  • Enter your new username (firstname.lastname@kry.se).
  • Enter your existing password and press Enter.
[ Screenshot: Login → Other user ]
03 / IMPACT ON YOUR APPS

What changes — and what doesn't

Most of your apps will continue working with no action needed. A small number of systems IT doesn't control may still show your old username — this is expected.

✓ Working as normal

  • Microsoft Teams — chats, calls, meetings
  • Outlook — email and calendar
  • SharePoint & OneDrive — files and folders
  • MyApps / SSO — most enterprise applications
  • VLS — handled automatically via SCIM
  • Quinyx — scheduling
  • Workday — HR system

! May still show your old username

  • SITH card systems — outside IT's control
  • Third-party systems you sign into with a separate username and password
  • Older bookmarks that include @ad.kry.se in the URL
04 / FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to change my password?
No. Your password stays exactly the same. Only the part after the "@" symbol in your username changes.
My email address changes — do I lose old emails?
No. Your mailbox, calendar, contacts and all historic emails are kept. The old @ad.kry.se address is kept as a secondary address so emails sent to your old address will still arrive in the same inbox.
What if I tap my existing profile instead of "Other user"?
The login will fail. Sign out and start again — this time, click "Other user" at the bottom-left and enter your new username (firstname.lastname@kry.se).
Do I need to do anything on my phone or the Authenticator app?
In most cases, no. You may be prompted to re-authenticate once in Teams or Outlook mobile — sign in with your new username when prompted.
Why is this change happening?
It simplifies your login: one consistent address (@kry.se / @kry.no) that matches your email. It also tidies up our identity setup behind the scenes, reducing confusion when working with external partners and connecting new applications.
Two of us share the same name — what happens?
A small number of users have a name conflict in the new domain. IT will contact you personally before your migration to agree the new username. You don't need to do anything until contacted.
An app still shows my old @ad.kry.se username — is something broken?
Probably not. Some third-party systems (especially anything outside Microsoft and your SSO) keep their own copy of your username. These can't always be updated by IT. If the app still works, no action is needed. If it stops working, raise a Zendesk ticket and we'll investigate.
